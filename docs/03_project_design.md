# Project Design

## Purpose

This document describes how the Community Health Outreach Project (CHOP) is designed to address the identified development problem.

---

# Project Goal

Improve access to primary healthcare services among rural households through community-based health outreach.

---

# Project Objectives

1. Increase community awareness of primary healthcare services.
2. Strengthen routine household visits by Community Health Workers.
3. Improve patient referral and follow-up.
4. Increase the use of primary healthcare services.

---

# Project Strategy

The project will work closely with Community Health Workers, village leaders, and local health facilities to provide regular household visits, community health education, early identification of health conditions, and timely referrals.

---

# Main Activities

* Train Community Health Workers.
* Conduct household visits.
* Deliver community health education sessions.
* Refer patients to nearby health facilities.
* Follow up referred patients.
* Monitor project performance using routine data.

---

# Expected Results

The project is expected to improve community awareness, strengthen referral systems, increase household access to health information, and improve the use of primary healthcare services.

---

# Success Factors

Project success will depend on:

* Active participation of Community Health Workers.
* Community engagement.
* Reliable data collection.
* Regular monitoring.
* Collaboration with local health facilities.

---

# Conclusion

The project design provides the foundation for developing the Results Framework, Monitoring and Evaluation System, and Impact Evaluation strategy.
