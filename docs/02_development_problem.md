# Development Problem

## Purpose

This document defines the main development problem identified during the situation analysis.

---

# Problem Statement

Many households in the target community do not access primary healthcare services on time. Delayed care seeking, limited community outreach, and weak follow-up of referred patients reduce the use of available healthcare services.

As a result, preventable health conditions may become more serious before people receive appropriate care.

---

# Root Causes

The situation analysis identified several factors contributing to the problem.

* Low community awareness of available health services.
* Limited household outreach by Community Health Workers.
* Weak referral follow-up.
* Long distance to health facilities.
* Limited use of health information for community decision-making.

---

# Effects

If these challenges continue, the community may experience:

* Low use of primary healthcare services.
* Delayed diagnosis and treatment.
* Increased preventable illnesses.
* Poor health outcomes among vulnerable groups.

---

# Project Response

The Community Health Outreach Project (CHOP) will address these challenges by strengthening community outreach, improving health education, supporting patient referrals, and promoting timely use of primary healthcare services.

---

# Conclusion

The identified development problem provides the basis for designing the project strategy, expected results, and Monitoring and Evaluation framework.
