# Theory of Change

## Purpose

This Theory of Change explains how the Community Health Outreach Project (CHOP) is expected to improve access to primary healthcare services in rural Tanzania.

It describes the logical pathway from project activities to outputs, outcomes, and long-term impact.

---

# Development Problem

Many people living in rural communities experience limited access to primary healthcare services due to low health awareness, delayed care seeking, and limited community outreach.

---

# Assumptions

The project assumes that:

* Community members are willing to participate.
* Community Health Workers receive adequate training.
* Health facilities are available to receive referred patients.
* Project activities are implemented as planned.
* Data collected through the project is accurate and reliable.

---

# Project Activities

* Train Community Health Workers.
* Conduct household visits.
* Deliver community health education.
* Screen community members for common health conditions.
* Refer patients to nearby health facilities.
* Follow up referred patients.
* Collect routine monitoring data.

---

# Outputs

The project is expected to produce:

* Community Health Workers trained.
* Household visits completed.
* Health education sessions conducted.
* Community members screened.
* Patients referred to health facilities.
* Routine monitoring data collected.

---

# Outcomes

The project is expected to contribute to:

* Increased awareness of primary healthcare services.
* Improved health-seeking behaviour.
* Increased completion of referrals.
* Increased use of primary healthcare services.
* Earlier detection of common health conditions.

---

# Long-Term Impact

Improved health outcomes and better access to primary healthcare services in rural communities.

---
