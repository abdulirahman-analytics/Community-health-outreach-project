# Theory of Change

## Overview

The Community Health Outreach Project (CHOP) is designed to improve access to primary healthcare services in rural Tanzania through community-based outreach. The project assumes that bringing health education and basic health services closer to households will encourage early care seeking and increase the use of health facilities.

---

## Development Problem

Many people living in rural communities have limited access to primary healthcare services due to low health awareness, delayed health-seeking behaviour, and limited community outreach.

---

## Inputs

* Project staff
* Community Health Workers
* Training materials
* Kobo Toolbox
* Mobile devices
* Financial resources
* Health facility collaboration

---

## Activities

* Train Community Health Workers.
* Conduct household visits.
* Deliver community health education.
* Screen community members for common health conditions.
* Refer patients to nearby health facilities.
* Follow up referred patients.
* Collect routine monitoring data.

---

## Outputs

* Community Health Workers trained.
* Household visits completed.
* Health education sessions conducted.
* Community members screened.
* Patients referred to health facilities.
* Routine monitoring data collected.

---

## Outcomes

### Short-Term Outcomes

* Increased health knowledge.
* Increased awareness of available health services.
* Improved community engagement.

### Medium-Term Outcomes

* Improved health-seeking behaviour.
* Increased completion of referrals.
* Increased utilization of primary healthcare services.

---

## Long-Term Impact

Improved access to primary healthcare services and better health outcomes in rural communities.

---

## Key Assumptions

* Community members are willing to participate.
* Community Health Workers receive adequate training.
* Health facilities are able to provide services.
* Project activities are implemented as planned.
* Project data is accurate and reliable.

---

## Risks

* Low community participation.
* Community Health Worker turnover.
* Stock-outs of medicines.
* Poor road access during rainy seasons.
* Delays in reporting project data.

---

## How This Supports the Project

This Theory of Change provides the logical foundation for the Results Framework, Logical Framework, Performance Measurement Framework, Monitoring and Evaluation Plan, and Impact Evaluation Design. It explains how project activities are expected to contribute to measurable changes and ultimately improve community health outcomes.
